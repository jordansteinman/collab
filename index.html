Determining Habitat Suitability for Kirtland's Warbler in South-Central Ontario

Project 2417

This project is being undertaken in partnership with the Oak Ridges Moraine Land Trust and the Nature Conservancy of Canada, but is being spearheaded by Fleming College's Forestry faculty for a National Sciences and Engineering Research Council grant. The Oak Ridges Moraine Land Trust is acquiring properties to be managed for Kirtland's Warbler (Endangered Species) habitat and our goal is to strategically prioritize those locations for greatest effectiveness. The Nature Conservancy of Canada is supporting this effort and will support the use of the "Where to Work" GIS software application that they produced with Carleton University. The group carrying out this work will be required to acquire and pre-process suitable files, determine spatial analysis approaches including habitat suitability indices and analogs. Finally, the deliverable will be a prioritization map with a description of the methodology.
